Supply & Demand — Basics

Supply and demand determine prices and quantities in markets. The intersection of the supply and demand curves is the market equilibrium.

Demand

Demand shows how much consumers want at each price. Generally, as price falls, quantity demanded rises (downward slope).

Supply

Supply shows how much producers will sell at each price. Generally, as price rises, quantity supplied rises (upward slope).

Equilibrium

Where supply and demand meet — that price clears the market (quantity supplied = quantity demanded).

Shifts vs movements

A change in price causes movement along a curve. A change in a non-price factor (income, tastes, input costs, technology) shifts the entire curve.

Elasticity

Elasticity measures how responsive quantity demanded or supplied is to price changes. High elasticity means quantity changes a lot with price.
