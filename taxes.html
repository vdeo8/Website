Taxes — Overview

Taxes fund public services and are collected at local, regional, and national levels. Here are common tax types and when they apply.

Income Tax

Collected on individual or corporate earnings. Many countries use progressive rates (higher income, higher rate). Used to fund general government services.

Payroll Tax

Withheld from wages to fund specific programs (e.g., social security, healthcare). Employers often contribute as well.

Sales Tax / VAT

Applied to the sale of goods and services. Sales tax is common in the US; VAT (value-added tax) is common in many other countries. Usually paid by consumers at purchase.

Property Tax

Levied on real estate based on assessed value. Typically used to fund local services like schools and roads.

Corporate Tax

Tax on company profits. Rates and rules vary widely by country and can affect investment and location decisions.

When are they used?

Different taxes fund different things: payroll taxes for social programs, property taxes for local services, income/corporate taxes for broader government spending, and sales taxes for consumption-based revenue.
